Doyle was born in 1859. He received a medical degree from the University of Edinburgh and began practicing in Southsea, England in 1882. He composed many stories, two of which were never published. In 1886, he created the Dr. Sherlock Holmes-inspired character. Joseph Bell, one of the lecturers. The first story, entitled A Study in Scarlet (Indonesian: Penelusuran Benang Merah) was well received by the public. However, the character's fame only began in 1891 when he wrote the adventure series Sherlock Holmes with his loyal friend, Dr. Watson, in the form of a compilation of short stories.
